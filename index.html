About

I’m David Chidester, a computer enthusiast and professional data scientist. I’m an enthusiastic computer geek and love Linux and open source software. I currently work as a data scientist and business analyst for Thermo Fisher Scientific, a bio-technology company whose mission is to enable its customers to make the world healthier and safer. I got my BA in computer science at Willamette University in Salem Oregon in May of 2019. I’ve previously worked in web development at Square 2 Design, building websites for clients. During my education at Willamette, I took classes in Simulations, Architecture and Compilers, and Data Science. I have also worked on independent projects in game development, android app development, and cyber security. When competing in the National Cyber League ethical hacking competition, I competed in the gold bracket, which is the top 15% of scorers during the pre-season. I have Asperger’s Syndrome (a high functioning form of Autism Spectrum Disorder) and have attended meetings for professionals on the Spectrum at AASCEND and have advocated for and mentored other young professionals with disabilities during my internship at JVS. While applying at Thermo Fisher I participated in the pilot of the Neurodiversity in the Workplace program. The goal of Neurodiversity in the Workplace is to prepare professionals with autism and other disabilities for their professional careers and to demonstrate to employers that having a neurodiverse workforce is worthwhile and beneficial. My dream is to one day make a meaningful contribution to the Linux kernel, no matter how small. Apart from computing, I also have a passion for cooking and I post pictures of my culinary creations on my Instagram page. In my free time I enjoy gaming, ultimate frisbee, and spending time with my dog, Shoko.
